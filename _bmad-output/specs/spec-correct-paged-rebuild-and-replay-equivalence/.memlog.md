- (direction) Express distillation selected for Story 1.14 from the implementation story, canonical epic criteria, PRD requirements FR7/FR33/FR36 and NFR7/NFR8/NFR16, architecture AD-20 and its governing invariants, and the current brownfield surfaces.
- (capability) CAP-1 — intent: Rebuild participants expose explicit full-replay or incremental semantics; success: no full-replay handler ever receives a lone page as a complete stream, and incremental handlers receive prior staged state plus one contiguous page.
- (capability) CAP-2 — intent: Paged rebuild work remains operation-scoped and non-live until every required projection is durably complete; success: incomplete, failed, or canceled work never replaces the last complete live actor/detail/index state.
- (capability) CAP-3 — intent: Paged and bounded rebuilds are position-equivalent to canonical aggregate replay; success: no event is duplicated, skipped, or reordered and the promoted result equals canonical replay through the same boundary.
- (capability) CAP-4 — intent: Operators can cancel, fail, and resume rebuilds without corrupting live state or overstating progress; success: retry resumes from a durable safe boundary and page-read progress is never reported as projection completion.
- (capability) CAP-5 — intent: Required projections promote as one logical operation while retaining distinguishable per-projection outcomes; success: the operation cannot succeed while any required projection is incomplete and rebuild checkpoints advance only after durable promotion.
- (capability) CAP-6 — intent: Projection lifecycle truthfully exposes rebuild progress; success: authoritative ProjectionBacked evidence is Rebuilding in flight and becomes Current or Stale only after durable promotion, with no terminal stale Rebuilding state.
- (capability) CAP-7 — intent: Replay-equivalence is demonstrated through persisted production-path evidence with bounded temporary full-prefix cost; success: a stream larger than two pages and the mandated edge corpus prove semantic equality of actor/detail/index outputs, freshness versions, and rebuild checkpoints, while limit exhaustion fails safely.
- (decision) Decision D1: the equivalence target is every live projection surface owned by the rebuild operation: projection-actor state, required detail/index read models, persisted freshness projection versions, and rebuild checkpoints; ETags are excluded as version evidence.
- (decision) Decision D2: under the current stateless Domain-keyed handler contract, page reads accumulate the complete required prefix to the bounded target, projection runs against that complete prefix, output remains operation-scoped and non-live, and promotion occurs only after all required outputs are complete.
- (decision) Decision D3: only IProjectionRebuildCheckpointStore advances after proven durable promotion; IProjectionCheckpointTracker delivery checkpoints remain unchanged for Story 1.13.
- (constraint) The existing immediate/poller full-replay path and admin rebuild control flow remain behaviorally compatible; this contract corrects paged rebuild semantics only.

- (constraint) Story 1.14 does not introduce Story 1.12 named asynchronous dispatch or Story 1.13 MessageId delivery deduplication; released interfaces remain source compatible and new capability is additive.
- (constraint) ProjectionOptions exposes a validated positive rebuild page size defaulting to 256; exact-boundary completion must remain correct for the configured value.
- (constraint) Full-prefix accumulation has a configured safety ceiling and structured fail-closed outcome; reaching the ceiling preserves live state, does not advance completion, and remains optimizable by Stories 6.3/6.4 without weakening equivalence.
- (constraint) Persisted freshness through IReadModelFreshness is the only projection-version authority; lifecycle is never inferred from ETag, HTTP outcome, payload fields, or SignalR.
